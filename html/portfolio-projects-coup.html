PROJECT

Coup, drive conversion to the valuation journey

MY ROLE

UX Research
UX Design

SUMMARY

I lead the UX research and UX process of the Coup project.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

We’re Coup, the faff-free service that makes finding, buying and selling a used car - easy

Currently, the Coup website displays the valuation banner poorly towards the bottom of the homepage that results to users struggle to find where they can get a valuation for their car. I gathered this evidence from the usability testing lab session I have conducted with my squad and from data recordings, and the majority of users struggled to find this feature to help them sell their car or just to get a valuation.

How do I help users to find where they can get a valuation for their car that leads to selling their car from the homepage?

PAIN POINTS - ASSUMPTIONS

Selling a car in general
My feeling on the way how people sell their cars can be a very painful process, the long process of selling privately i.e advertising, viewing, interacting etc. Part-exchanging for less money and being under pressure. Selling to third party companies could potentially be much less with poor customer experience.

Selling a car on Coup
User experience could be improved a whole lot more to help users through the process of selling their car. The valuation feature isn't clear and prominent on the current homepage especially on mobile.

WHAT IF?

I ask myself what if I make the process that much easier and a better experience, would that encourage users to change their car more often? What are people’s general feelings about selling their car? What is the process of selling their car? What do people think of selling their cars to third party companies? These are some of the questions I needed validations.

AFFINITY MAPPING

With the questions I had in mind, I decided to facilitate an Infinity Mapping session with my squad to identify what are the key areas that customers do in order to sell their cars. The below left image shows the prioritisation of what my team and myself thinks users would do to sell their cars.

DEFINE GOALS

By defining the goals will help me find out what I am trying to do and learn. This is where I will reflect on the notes from the interviews and usability testing to help collect data that answer the questions.

I want to discover:
- The users’ pain points, needs, and goals when selling a car
- The users’ processes when selling a car
- How users currently using the website to value their car
- What users think Coup offers them (to see if the basic concept of the site is clear)
- What do users think about selling their car to a third party company

FURTHER UNDERSTANDING

Who do I need to learn this from?
To be able to understand this, I will need to speak to users who had experience, are in the process of selling their car and thinking of selling their car

How will I use the data I've gathered?
To identify the pain points users have when going through the valuation process and to find the solutions to make the process a greater user experience so that users have the confidence to move forward in either selling their car or to browse cars on the website.

OKRs (OJECTIVE KEY RESULTS)

I have created a user story with the goal in mind, that’s to make the process of valuating cars a much more enjoyable and delightful process. This is also to help me refer back to the problems I am trying to solve.

As a user, I would like…
A way to help me get a valuation and sell my car easily without the long and painful process…

UX RESEARCH

User interview - The purpose
User interviews will help me understand what the user thinks and feels, to empathise with the users. This gives myself a bigger picture to identify the pain points and map out the requirements for the feature(s). It also helps me to understand the emotions and body language that adds more details to the research.

Usability testing - The purpose
The purpose of this session is to find the problems users encounter when they are going through the selling process and to find out further how they feel about it. Creating a list of scenarios for users to do. These will reveal important information relevant to the subject we are testing.

Scenarios
I asked participants to look around the homepage and see what they make of it. So, what they think they could do on here, what the site is and who’s the site is for etc

I also asked participants to complete different tasks of how they might go through the valuation journey. Whether it’s helping a family member to get a valuation of their car, a recommendation, and applying damage for a more accurate valuation. These then follow up with multiple-choice questions.

Data Recordings
I have added tracking to record the current flow of the valuation journey using Hotjar, to see how users interact with it and to see what sort of information I can learn from the recordings.

Usability testing
From the research gathered, it has identified the pain points many users have going through the flow. Some would decide to leave after several tries in within the journey. Content and the flow of the valuation journey is confusing and on mobile, has proven most difficult to use.

All users went to the hamburger navigation menu to start the valuation process which took some time to find except for 1 user on mobile. On mobile the valuation feature is hidden behind the hamburger menu, therefore, it will require users to tap twice to find it and the feature isn’t visible unless you scroll down below the fold.

The ‘What’s my car worth?’ (valuation banner) copy isn’t clear and the banner doesn’t stand out enough. From the findings, some users saw the banner but didn’t click on it because they weren’t sure if it was to valuate, sell or just finding out how much your car is worth. Due to the inconsistency of copy within the banner.

This has validated my initial assumptions of pain points users have when getting a valuation for their car.
